Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current supply gap in Nigeria's palm oil industry?

+

Nigeria currently faces a supply deficit of approximately 500,000 metric tons annually. The country produces about 1.2 million metric tons but consumes 1.7 million metric tons, requiring significant imports to meet domestic demand.

What are the investment opportunities in the sector?

+

Key investment opportunities include: modern processing mills (5-60 tons/hour capacity), high-yield seedling nurseries, digital platforms for farm management, and sustainable farming technology. Expected ROI ranges from 25-50% depending on the investment type.

How can technology help bridge the supply gap?

+

Modern technology can increase oil extraction rates from 15-18% to 23-25%, reduce processing time by 95%, lower labor requirements by 70%, and improve energy efficiency by 60%. These improvements can significantly boost domestic production capacity.

What states in Nigeria produce the most palm oil?

+

The top producing states are: Akwa Ibom (24% of national production), Rivers (18%), Delta (15%), Edo (13%), and Bayelsa (10%). These states have favorable climatic conditions and established palm oil farming traditions.

How can I get involved in the industry revitalization?

+

You can get involved through: direct investment in processing facilities, partnership with smallholder farmers, technology provision and training, research collaboration, or supporting policy advocacy. Contact us to discuss specific opportunities that match your interests and resources.
